RECITALS

A. The Founder desires to establish a trust for the benefit of his family and descendants, providing for the preservation and growth of family assets, succession planning, and the protection of assets from creditor claims.

B. The Trustees have agreed to act as trustees of the trust on the terms and conditions set out herein.

C. The trust is established in accordance with the provisions of the Trustee Act (Act No. 57 of 1951) of Namibia and the common law applicable in Namibia.

---

1. ESTABLISHMENT AND NAME

1.1 The Founder hereby establishes a trust to be known as the STEENKAMP FAMILY TRUST (hereinafter referred to as "the Trust").

1.2 The Trust is a discretionary trust and shall be governed by the laws of the Republic of Namibia.

---

2. TRUST PROPERTY

2.1 The Founder hereby donates and transfers to the Trust the initial trust property consisting of:
    (a) A cash donation of NAD 100 (one hundred Namibian dollars); and
    (b) Such further assets as may be donated, sold, or otherwise transferred to the Trust from time to time.

2.2 The trustees are authorised to accept donations, inheritances, bequests, and any other assets from any person for the benefit of the Trust.

2.3 The trust assets shall include, but are not limited to:
    (a) Farm Doringhoek 247, Hardap Region, measuring 3,842 hectares, registered in the Deeds Registry under Title Deed T. 4821/2003;
    (b) Livestock and farming equipment as listed in Schedule A hereto;
    (c) A 60% shareholding in Steenkamp Agri Holdings (Pty) Ltd (Reg. No. 2014/1129/07);
    (d) A fixed deposit account with Bank Windhoek, Account No. 62 4419 8833, current balance NAD 2,340,000.

---

3. OBJECTS OF THE TRUST

3.1 The primary objects of the Trust are:
    (a) To hold, manage, and administer trust assets for the benefit of the beneficiaries;
    (b) To provide for the financial security, education, health, and general welfare of the beneficiaries;
    (c) To preserve and grow the family assets for the benefit of future generations;
    (d) To engage in any lawful activity for the purpose of generating income for the benefit of the beneficiaries.

---

4. TRUSTEES

4.1 Appointment and Removal
    (a) The minimum number of trustees shall be two (2) at all times.
    (b) A trustee may be removed by resolution of the remaining trustees if such trustee:
        (i) becomes insolvent or is sequestrated;
        (ii) is convicted of a criminal offence involving dishonesty;
        (iii) is declared mentally incapacitated by a court of competent jurisdiction;
        (iv) fails to perform his or her duties in accordance with this deed.
    (c) Vacancies in the office of trustee shall be filled by the remaining trustees by written resolution.

4.2 Powers of Trustees
    The trustees shall have the following powers, which they must exercise jointly and in the best interests of the beneficiaries:
    (a) To purchase, sell, lease, mortgage, or otherwise deal with any immovable or movable property;
    (b) To invest trust funds in any investment permitted by the Trustee Act;
    (c) To open and operate bank accounts in the name of the Trust;
    (d) To borrow money on such terms as the trustees deem fit, provided that any borrowing exceeding NAD 500,000 shall require unanimous trustee consent;
    (e) To appoint professional advisers including attorneys, accountants, and investment managers;
    (f) To institute, defend, or settle legal proceedings on behalf of the Trust;
    (g) To distribute income and capital to beneficiaries at their discretion.

4.3 Trustee Remuneration
    (a) The Independent Professional Trustee (Edgepoint Trust (Namibia) (Pty) Ltd) shall be entitled to remuneration in accordance with its published fee schedule as amended from time to time.
    (b) Individual trustees who are also beneficiaries shall not be entitled to remuneration unless otherwise resolved by a majority of trustees.

4.4 Meetings and Resolutions
    (a) Trustees shall meet at least once per calendar year.
    (b) Resolutions may also be passed by written resolution signed by all trustees.
    (c) A quorum shall consist of at least two trustees.
    (d) Decisions shall be by majority vote save where this deed requires unanimity.

---

5. BENEFICIARIES

5.1 The trustees shall have full and unfettered discretion to determine:
    (a) Which beneficiaries shall receive distributions;
    (b) The amounts and timing of such distributions;
    (c) Whether distributions shall be from income or capital.

5.2 No beneficiary shall have a vested right to any specific distribution from the Trust.

5.3 The trustees shall give due consideration to the needs, circumstances, and best interests of each beneficiary when exercising their discretion.

5.4 Minor beneficiaries shall not receive distributions directly. Distributions for their benefit shall be held in trust or paid to their natural guardian until they attain the age of 21 years.

---

6. DURATION AND TERMINATION

6.1 The Trust shall continue in perpetuity unless terminated in accordance with clause 6.2.

6.2 The Trust shall terminate upon:
    (a) A resolution by all trustees and the majority of adult beneficiaries;
    (b) An order of a competent court; or
    (c) The total distribution of all trust assets.

6.3 Upon termination, the remaining trust assets shall be distributed to the beneficiaries in such proportions as the trustees determine in their discretion.

---

7. GOVERNING LAW

This deed shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Republic of Namibia. Any disputes arising hereunder shall be submitted to the jurisdiction of the High Court of Namibia.
